<p><p>The chemical sensors market is characterized by several types, each designed for specific applications. Electrochemical sensors detect chemical changes via electrical signals, commonly used in gas monitoring. Optical sensors utilize light to detect chemical concentrations, offering high sensitivity. Semiconductor sensors employ conductive materials to sense gases and vapors. Pellistor or catalytic bead sensors measure combustibles by heating a catalyst, while 'others' encompass various novel technologies. Collectively, these sensors play crucial roles in environmental monitoring, industrial processes, and safety applications.</p></p>
