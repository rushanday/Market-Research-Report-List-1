<p><ul><li>Bio-Inert Ceramics</li><li>Bioactive Ceramics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The ceramics biomaterials market comprises primarily two types: bio-inert ceramics and bioactive ceramics. Bio-inert ceramics, such as alumina and zirconia, are characterized by their compatibility and stability in biological environments, often used in implants due to their mechanical strength. Conversely, bioactive ceramics, like hydroxyapatite, promote bone growth and bonding with tissue through chemical interaction. These materials are essential in applications like dental implants and orthopedic devices, enhancing healing and integration within the body.</p></p>
