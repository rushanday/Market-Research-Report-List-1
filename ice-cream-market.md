<p><p>The ice cream market comprises primarily two types: soft ice cream and hard ice cream. Soft ice cream is characterized by its light, airy texture and is typically served directly from a machine, making it creamy and easy to swirl. In contrast, hard ice cream is denser and more rigid, requiring scooping for serving. While soft ice cream is often associated with quick-service environments like ice cream trucks or parlors, hard ice cream is favored for its versatility in various dessert applications and retail settings.</p></p>
