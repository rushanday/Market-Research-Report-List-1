<p><strong>The Respiratory Antivirals Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Nucleoside Analogs</li><li>Neuraminidase Inhibitors</li><li>Ion Channel Blockers</li><li>Fusion Protein Inhibitors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The respiratory antivirals market includes several key types: </p><p>1. **Nucleoside Analogs**: These mimic natural nucleosides, inhibiting viral replication by interfering with RNA and DNA synthesis. </p><p>2. **Neuraminidase Inhibitors**: These block the neuraminidase enzyme, preventing the release of new viral particles from infected cells. </p><p>3. **Ion Channel Blockers**: These disrupt viral replication by blocking ion channels, thereby altering the cellular environment. </p><p>4. **Fusion Protein Inhibitors**: These target viral fusion processes, preventing the virus from entering host cells. Each type plays a crucial role in managing respiratory viral infections.</p></p>
