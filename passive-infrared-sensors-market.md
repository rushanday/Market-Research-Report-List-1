<p><p>Passive Infrared Sensors (PIR) operate by detecting infrared radiation emitted by objects within their field of view. The market is primarily segmented into two types: thermal and quantum sensors. Thermal PIR sensors utilize pyroelectric materials to sense temperature changes caused by moving objects, making them ideal for applications like motion detection and security systems. Quantum PIR sensors, on the other hand, leverage advanced semiconductor technology to achieve higher sensitivity and accuracy, typically used in specialized applications like imaging and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
